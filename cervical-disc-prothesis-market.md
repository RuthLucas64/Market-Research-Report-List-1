<p><p>Cervical disc prosthesis options include metal-on-metal, metal-on-polymer, and other materials. Metal-on-metal devices feature metallic surfaces that provide durability and resistance to wear but may pose risks of metal ion release. Metal-on-polymer implants combine metal components with polymer surfaces to reduce friction and enhance patient comfort, while also minimizing wear-related issues. Other types may incorporate advanced materials like ceramics or composite structures to offer different mechanical properties and biocompatibility, catering to diverse patient needs and preferences.</p></p>
