<p><p>The textile fiber dye market encompasses various types of dyes tailored for specific fibers and applications. Disperse dyes are primarily used for synthetic fibers like polyester, offering vibrant colors. Reactive dyes bond chemically with fiber, making them ideal for cotton and other cellulose materials. Sulfur dyes provide excellent washfastness and are suited for dark shades on cotton. Vat dyes are used for their color permanence, especially in indigo. Acid dyes are favored for protein fibers like silk and wool, while "other dyes" include specialty dyes for unique applications.</p></p>
