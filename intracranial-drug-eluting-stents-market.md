<p><p>Intracranial drug-eluting stents are specialized medical devices designed for the treatment of conditions such as intracranial atherosclerotic disease. These stents are coated with drugs that reduce the risk of restenosis, or the re-narrowing of blood vessels, thereby improving patient outcomes. The integration of drug-eluting technology into stents provides enhanced therapeutic benefits compared to traditional bare-metal stents, making them an appealing option for neurosurgeons and interventional radiologists.</p><p>The market for intracranial drug-eluting stents is witnessing significant growth due to increasing incidences of neurovascular disorders and advancements in stent design and drug delivery systems. Moreover, rising awareness about minimally invasive procedures is driving demand for these advanced therapeutic solutions. The growing aging population, coupled with higher rates of diabetes and hypertension, contributes to the increasing prevalence of conditions necessitating intracranial interventions.</p><p>Latest trends indicate a focus on developing bioresorbable drug-eluting stents and innovations in drug formulations to enhance efficacy and safety.
