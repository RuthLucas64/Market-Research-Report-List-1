<p><p>The plastic chip card market primarily comprises two types: contact cards and contactless cards. Contact cards require a physical connection to a card reader, enabling secure transactions through embedded chips. They are commonly used in financial and identification applications. In contrast, contactless cards utilize radio frequency identification (RFID) technology, allowing users to tap their cards without direct contact. This promotes quicker transactions and enhanced convenience. Both card types are widely adopted for secure payments and data storage in various industries.</p></p>
