<p><p>Polymer materials used for drug packaging include PVC, PE, PET, and PP, each offering distinct benefits. PVC (polyvinyl chloride) is known for its durability and resistance to moisture, making it ideal for various pharmaceutical applications. PE (polyethylene) provides flexibility and a barrier against chemicals, enhancing the longevity of drug products. PET (polyethylene terephthalate) is lightweight and recyclable, while PP (polypropylene) exhibits high chemical resistance and thermal stability. Other polymers may include materials like PS (polystyrene) or multi-layer structures, further optimizing packaging solutions.</p></p>
