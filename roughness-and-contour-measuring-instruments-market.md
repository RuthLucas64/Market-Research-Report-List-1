<p><p>The roughness and contour measuring instruments market is divided into two main types: contact and non-contact. Contact instruments utilize tactile sensors to directly touch the surface for precise roughness and contour measurements, providing detailed data on surface texture. In contrast, non-contact instruments employ optical or laser technologies to capture surface profiles without physical contact, making them ideal for delicate or complex surfaces. Both types serve essential roles in manufacturing and quality control across various industries, ensuring product integrity and performance.</p></p>
