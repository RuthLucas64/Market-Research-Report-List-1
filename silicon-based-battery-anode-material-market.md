<p><p>The silicon-based battery anode material market encompasses two primary types: SiO/C and Si/C. SiO/C (silicon oxide/carbon) combines silicon oxide with a carbon matrix, enhancing electrochemical performance and stability while reducing volume expansion during charging cycles. Si/C (silicon/carbon) utilizes silicon particles within a carbon structure, maximizing energy density and improving conductivity. Both materials aim to overcome the limitations of traditional graphite anodes, enabling the development of higher-capacity lithium-ion batteries for various applications, including electric vehicles and portable electronics.</p></p>
