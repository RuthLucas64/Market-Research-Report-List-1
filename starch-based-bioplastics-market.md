<p><p>The starch-based bioplastics market comprises various processing types, including blow molding, extrusion, injection molding, and others such as thermoforming and foaming. Blow molding shapes bioplastics into hollow objects, while extrusion creates continuous shapes by forcing material through a die. Injection molding allows for precise, complex designs by injecting molten bioplastic into molds. Thermoforming involves heating sheets of bioplastics and shaping them using molds, whereas foaming produces lightweight structures by incorporating gas into the material, enhancing insulation and cushioning properties.</p></p>
